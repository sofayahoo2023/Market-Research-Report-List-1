<p><p>Protein detection and quantitation refers to the process of identifying and measuring proteins in a sample. There are two main types of markets in this field: instrument detection and reagent detection. Instrument detection involves the use of specialized devices, such as mass spectrometers or chromatography systems, to detect and quantify proteins. Reagent detection, on the other hand, relies on the use of specific reagents or chemicals that react with proteins, producing a measurable signal. Both types of markets offer different approaches for protein detection and quantitation, catering to different research needs and applications.</p></p>
