<p><p>Oncology-based molecular diagnostics refers to the use of molecular biological techniques to detect and analyze genetic alterations in cancer cells. Various types of molecular diagnostic methods are used in this field. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) is a technique that amplifies and detects specific DNA sequences. Multiplex PCR allows the amplification of multiple target genes simultaneously. In Situ Hybridization (ISH) visualizes specific genes or chromosomes in intact cells. Isothermal Nucleic Acid Amplification Technology (INAAT) amplifies nucleic acids at a constant temperature without the need for thermal cycling. These techniques play a crucial role in understanding cancer biology and developing personalized treatments.</p></p>
