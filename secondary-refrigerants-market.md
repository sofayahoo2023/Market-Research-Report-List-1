<p><p>Secondary refrigerants are substances used in refrigeration systems to transfer heat from one place to another. The market for secondary refrigerants can be categorized into different types, including glycols, salt brines, carbon dioxide, and others. Glycols, like propylene and ethylene glycol, have good heat transfer and antifreeze properties. Salt brines, such as calcium chloride and potassium acetate, have low freezing points and are commonly used in cold storage facilities. Carbon dioxide, being a low-cost and eco-friendly option, is gaining popularity. Other secondary refrigerants may include ammonia, hydrocarbons, and refrigerant blends.</p></p>
