<p><p>Vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty are minimally invasive procedures used to treat spinal fractures, particularly those caused by osteoporosis. In these procedures, a small incision is made in the back and a special device is used to inject bone cement into the fractured vertebrae. Vertebroplasty involves direct injection of the cement, while kyphoplasty uses a balloon-like device to create space before the cement is injected.</p><p>The vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty device market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The increasing prevalence of osteoporosis and spinal fractures, along with the growing geriatric population, is driving the demand for these procedures. Additionally, technological advancements in the devices used for vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty are also contributing to market growth.</p><p>The market is also benefiting from the rising awareness among healthcare professionals and patients about the benefits of minimally invasive procedures compared to traditional open surgeries.
