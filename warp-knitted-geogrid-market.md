<p><ul><li>Biaxial Tension</li><li>Uniaxial Tension</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Warp knitted geogrids are a type of geosynthetic material used in civil engineering applications. They are classified into two main market types: Biaxial Tension and Uniaxial Tension.</p><p>Biaxial Tension geogrids are designed to withstand tensile forces applied in two perpendicular directions, providing strength and stability to reinforced soils and aggregate layers. These geogrids are commonly used in applications such as pavement construction and soil stabilization.</p><p>On the other hand, Uniaxial Tension geogrids are specifically engineered to bear tensile forces in only one direction. They are primarily used to reinforce slopes and retaining walls, ensuring stability and preventing soil erosion.</p><p>Both market types of warp knitted geogrids play essential roles in various construction projects, offering different solutions for reinforcement depending on the specific needs of each application.</p></p>
