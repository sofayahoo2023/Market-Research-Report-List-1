<p><p>Epoxy-based conductive adhesives are a type of adhesive commonly used in electronic applications to bond components together while also providing electrical conductivity. The market for these adhesives can be classified into two types: one-component conductive adhesives and two-component conductive adhesives. </p><p>One-component conductive adhesives come ready to use and do not require any mixing before application. They are convenient and time-saving, making them a popular choice for electronic assembly processes. </p><p>In contrast, two-component conductive adhesives consist of two separate components that need to be mixed together before application. This allows for better control over adhesive properties, such as viscosity and cure time, resulting in stronger bonds and improved performance.</p><p>Both types of epoxy-based conductive adhesives offer unique advantages and are widely used in various industries for their ability to provide both adhesion and electrical conductivity.</p></p>
