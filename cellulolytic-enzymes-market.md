<p><p>Cellulolytic enzymes are a group of enzymes that break down cellulose, which is a complex carbohydrate found in plants. The EG (endoglucanase) market focuses on enzymes that cleave internal bonds within the cellulose molecule. The CBH (cellobiohydrolase) market includes enzymes that cleave cellulose from the ends, producing cellobiose. Lastly, the BG (beta-glucosidase) market consists of enzymes that convert cellobiose into glucose. These enzymes play a crucial role in the production of biofuels, as they enable the breakdown of cellulose into simple sugars, which can then be fermented into fuel.</p></p>
