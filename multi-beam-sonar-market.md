<p><p>Multi-beam sonar is a type of underwater sonar technology that uses multiple beams to generate a detailed image of the seafloor or underwater objects. It works by emitting a fan-shaped beam of sound waves that is wider than traditional single-beam sonar systems. The beams are then reflected back to the sonar transducer, which collects the data and generates a high-resolution image.</p><p>The multi-beam sonar market is experiencing significant growth due to its wide range of applications. It is extensively used in various industries such as oil and gas, defense, underwater archaeology, hydrographic surveying, and marine biology, among others. The technology's ability to provide detailed and accurate underwater images is driving its adoption in these sectors.</p><p>The market analysis indicates that the increasing demand for underwater mapping and exploration activities is one of the key factors driving the growth of the multi-beam sonar market.
