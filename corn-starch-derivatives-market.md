<p><p>Corn starch derivatives are widely used in various industries such as food, pharmaceutical, and cosmetic industries. The types of corn starch derivatives in the market include maltodextrin, cyclodextrin, glucose syrup, spray dried starch, and hydrolysates. Maltodextrin is commonly used as a thickening agent, while cyclodextrin is used to encapsulate and deliver active ingredients. Glucose syrup is a sweetener used in food production, while spray dried starch is used in powdered food products. Hydrolysates are enzymatically hydrolyzed corn starch derivatives used in infant formulas and medical nutritional products.</p></p>
