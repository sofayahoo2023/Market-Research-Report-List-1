<p><p>Analog-to-Digital Converters (ADC) are electronic devices used to convert analog signals into digital form. The market for ADCs is diverse and includes various types, such as Pipeline ADC, SAR ADC, Sigma-Delta ADC, Flash ADC, and Others. Pipeline ADC divides the conversion process into smaller stages for higher speed and accuracy. SAR ADC uses successive approximation to convert the analog signal. Sigma-Delta ADC oversamples the analog signal and uses a feedback loop for high resolution. Flash ADC uses a series of comparators to rapidly convert the signal. The "Others" category includes different ADC types that do not fit into the mentioned types.</p></p>
