<p><p>A self-service book lending machine, also known as an automated library system or a library kiosk, is a technological solution that allows library users to borrow and return books without the need for human intervention. Self-service book lending machines are designed to enhance the efficiency and convenience of library services, allowing users to access books at any time, even when the library is closed.</p><p>The future of the self-service book lending machine market looks promising. With the increasing adoption of automation and digitization in various industries, it is expected that more libraries will implement these machines to streamline their operations and improve user experience. Infrared touch screens use infrared light to detect and track touch inputs, making them suitable for outdoor use and resistant to dust and water. On the other hand, capacitive touch screens respond to the electrical charge of a user's touch, offering enhanced clarity and sensitivity. Both technologies provide seamless user experiences and contribute to the efficiency of self-service book lending machines.</p></p>
