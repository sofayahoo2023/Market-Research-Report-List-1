<p><p>Diesel vehicles NOx sensors are classified into two market types: Five Needles and Four Needles. Five Needles refers to NOx sensors that measure five gases, including NOx, oxygen, temperature, pressure, and humidity. These sensors provide more comprehensive and accurate data for effective emission control. On the other hand, Four Needles NOx sensors measure only four gases, excluding humidity measurement. Although Four Needles sensors provide less detailed information, they are still reliable for monitoring and controlling NOx emissions in diesel vehicles.</p></p>

<p><strong>What are the Emerging Trends in the Global Diesel Vehicles NOx Sensors market?</strong></p>
<p><p>The global diesel vehicles NOx sensors market is witnessing certain emerging trends. One such trend is the increasing adoption of selective catalytic reduction (SCR) systems in diesel vehicles to control and reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions. Another emerging trend is the development of advanced NOx sensing technologies, such as tunable diode laser absorption spectroscopy (TDLAS) and chemiluminescence sensors, which offer high sensitivity and accuracy for detecting and measuring NOx levels.
