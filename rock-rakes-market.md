Rotary rock rakes feature spinning tines or blades that penetrate the ground and gather rocks, while also breaking up clumps of soil. They are popular for their ability to cover large areas quickly. Rigid rock rakes, on the other hand, have fixed tines and are typically more durable and heavy-duty. They are suitable for tougher terrains and can withstand considerable pressure. Both types serve the purpose of enhancing soil quality by removing unwanted rocks and preparing the ground for cultivation.</p></p>
