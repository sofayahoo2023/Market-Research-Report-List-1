<p><p>Tannin is a naturally occurring organic substance primarily found in plant tissues, bark, fruits, and leaves. It is widely used in various industries such as food and beverages, leather tanning, wine production, and pharmaceuticals, owing to its astringent and antioxidant properties. Tannins have the ability to bind and precipitate proteins, making them useful in the clarification and preservation of beverages such as wine and beer. In leather tanning, tannin is used to convert animal hides into leather by binding with collagen fibers. Tannins are also known for their medicinal properties, such as reducing inflammation and preventing microbial growth.</p><p>The tannin market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 6.4% during the forecast period.
