<p><p>Medium Density Polyethylene (MDPE) is a type of polyethylene with a density range of 0.926 to 0.940 g/cm³. It is widely used in various industries for its excellent balance of stiffness, toughness, and environmental stress crack resistance. MDPE can be produced through two different processes, namely Autoclave Process and Tubular Process. In the Autoclave Process, a high-pressure reactor is used to polymerize ethylene, while the Tubular Process involves the condensation of ethylene gas with a catalyst in a series of tubular reactors. Both processes are used to produce MDPE with different properties to cater to specific market needs.</p></p>
