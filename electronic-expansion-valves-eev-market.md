<p><p>Electronic Expansion Valves (EEVs) are devices used to control the flow of refrigerant in air conditioning and refrigeration systems. They can be classified into two market types: Electromagnetic EEVs and Electric EEVs. Electromagnetic EEVs use a solenoid coil to regulate refrigerant flow, ensuring precise control and high responsiveness. On the other hand, Electric EEVs utilize stepper motors or servo motors to adjust the valve opening, allowing for accurate modulation. Both types offer advantages in terms of energy efficiency, temperature control, and system stability, catering to different applications and customer preferences in the market.</p></p>
