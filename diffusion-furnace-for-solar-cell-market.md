<p><p>Diffusion furnaces are used in the solar cell manufacturing process to introduce impurities into the silicon wafer, which enhances its electrical properties. There are two main types of diffusion furnaces: horizontal and vertical furnaces. Horizontal furnaces have a flat configuration, where the wafers are placed horizontally on a boat and moved through a high-temperature zone. Vertical furnaces, on the other hand, have a vertical configuration, and the wafers are loaded in a standing position and move up and down through multiple temperature zones. Both types are widely used in the solar cell industry for different manufacturing needs.</p></p>
