<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Automotive UV sensors can be categorized into three types based on the specific wavelength range of ultraviolet (UV) radiation they are designed to detect: UVA, UVB, and UVC sensors. UVA sensors detect UV radiation with wavelengths ranging from 320 to 400 nm, which are primarily responsible for skin tanning and aging. UVB sensors detect UV radiation with wavelengths between 280 and 320 nm, which can cause sunburn and play a role in the development of skin cancer. UVC sensors detect highly energetic UV radiation with wavelengths between 100 and 280 nm, which are harmful to humans and are typically filtered out by the atmosphere.</p></p>
