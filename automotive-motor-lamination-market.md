<p><p>The automotive motor lamination market types include Cold Rolled Non-Oriented (CRNO) and Cold Rolled Grain-Oriented (CRGO). CRNO refers to motor lamination made from steel sheets that are cold-rolled to maintain a uniform thickness, making it suitable for various types of motors. On the other hand, CRGO refers to motor lamination made from steel sheets that are cold-rolled in a specific direction to align the magnetic grains, reducing energy loss and improving motor efficiency. Both types of market offer different benefits depending on the requirements of the motor application.</p></p>
