<p><p>Antifog additives are used to prevent the formation of fog or condensation on various surfaces, such as lenses, mirrors, and packaging materials. The market for antifog additives can be categorized into four types: glycerol esters, polyglycerol esters, sorbitan esters of fatty acids, and ethoxylated sorbitan esters. Glycerol esters are derived from glycerin and fatty acids, while polyglycerol esters are synthesized from glycerol and fatty acids. Sorbitan esters of fatty acids are obtained from sorbitol and fatty acids, and ethoxylated sorbitan esters are produced by combining sorbitan esters with ethylene oxide. These additives play a crucial role in preventing fogging and ensuring clarity in various applications.</p></p>
