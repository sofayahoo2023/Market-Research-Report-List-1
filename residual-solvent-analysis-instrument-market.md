<p><p>The market for residual solvent analysis instruments can be categorized into two types: Gas Chromatography (GC) and Gas Chromatography Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS). GC is a technique that separates chemical compounds to measure the amount of residual solvents present in a sample. On the other hand, GC-MS combines the separation power of GC with the identification ability of mass spectrometry. This allows for more accurate and precise analysis of residual solvents. Both types of instruments play a crucial role in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, food, and environmental testing.</p></p>
