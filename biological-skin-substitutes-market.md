Human donor tissue-derived products are derived from human cadavers and are used as skin grafts. Acellular animal-derived products are derived from animal sources and have the cellular components removed, making them safer for use. Biosynthetic products are made in the laboratory and mimic the properties of natural skin. All these products are used as substitutes for damaged or lost skin in various medical procedures.</p></p>
