<p><p>Carbon Carbon Composites (C/C composites) are a type of advanced material known for their exceptional strength, stiffness, and high-temperature resistance. The market for C/C composites can be broadly categorized into two types: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process (LIP). CVD involves depositing carbon layers on a carbon fiber substrate to create a composite with precise control over thickness and properties. LIP, on the other hand, employs liquid resin impregnation into the carbon fiber preform, followed by densification through heat treatments. Both processes contribute to the production and supply of high-quality C/C composites in various industries.</p></p>
