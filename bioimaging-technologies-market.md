<p><p>Bioimaging technologies refer to various imaging techniques that enable visualization of biological structures and processes. Medical bio-imaging encompasses all types of imaging used in medical diagnostics and research. Optical imaging uses light to create images of tissues and cells. Radiological imaging includes X-rays, CT scans, and PET scans. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) utilizes magnetic fields and radio waves to generate detailed images of internal organs. Ultrasound imaging employs sound waves for visualizing internal structures. Molecular bio-imaging enables visualization of specific molecules within biological systems. Nano bio-imaging involves imaging at a nanoscale level. Biomarkers and molecular probes are used to identify and track specific molecules in biological samples.</p></p>
