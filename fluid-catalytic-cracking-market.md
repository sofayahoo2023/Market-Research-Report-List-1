<p><p>Fluid Catalytic Cracking (FCC) is a vital process in the petroleum refining industry that converts heavy oil fractions into lighter, more valuable products. The FCC market has different types, including side-by-side and stacked-type configurations. Side-by-side type refers to an arrangement where the regenerator is placed beside the reactor, allowing for a compact and streamlined design. Stacked-type, on the other hand, involves a vertical structure where the regenerator is positioned above the reactor, providing more flexibility in terms of heat integration and catalyst circulation. These configurations cater to varying operational needs and preferences of refineries.</p></p>
