<p><p>Hemodynamic monitoring devices are used to measure and assess the performance of the cardiovascular system. There are three types of hemodynamic monitoring devices: invasive, minimally invasive, and non-invasive. Invasive devices involve the insertion of a catheter into a blood vessel, providing direct measurements such as blood pressure and cardiac output. Minimally invasive devices use less invasive techniques like pulse contour analysis to estimate hemodynamic parameters. Non-invasive devices do not require any invasive procedures and measure hemodynamic parameters using external sensors or technologies like ultrasound or pulse oximetry.</p></p>
