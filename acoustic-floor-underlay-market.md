<p><p>Acoustic floor underlay is a material that is used to reduce noise in buildings by dampening the sound that is transmitted through the floor. It is typically made from materials such as rubber, foam, or cork, and it is installed underneath the flooring material to provide an additional layer of insulation. The main purpose of acoustic floor underlay is to minimize the impact sound (footsteps, furniture moving) and the airborne sound (voices, music) that can be transmitted between floors in a building.</p><p>The global acoustic floor underlay market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for noise reduction in residential and commercial buildings, coupled with stringent building regulations regarding noise pollution, is driving the market growth.
