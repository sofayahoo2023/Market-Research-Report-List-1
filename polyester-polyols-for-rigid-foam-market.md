<p><ul><li>Vacuum Melting Method</li><li>Carrier Gas Melting Method</li><li>Azeotropic Vapor Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyester Polyols for Rigid Foam Market Types are characterized by three methods: Vacuum Melting Method, Carrier Gas Melting Method, and Azeotropic Vapor Method. In the Vacuum Melting Method, polyester polyols are melted under vacuum conditions, resulting in reduced air and moisture content. The Carrier Gas Melting Method involves using inert gas as a carrier to melt the polyols, leading to enhanced stability and lower production costs. Lastly, the Azeotropic Vapor Method employs azeotropic distillation to remove water from the polyols, improving their quality and performance. These methods offer different advantages in the production of polyester polyols for rigid foam.</p></p>
